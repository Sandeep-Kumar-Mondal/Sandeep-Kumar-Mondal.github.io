Research

Research Overview

My research focuses on understanding the physical processes governing emission from relativistic jets in Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN), using multi-wavelength data and theoretical modeling. I investigate particle acceleration, radiation mechanisms, and their possible connection to high-energy neutrinos.

Highlighted Research

Spectral Modeling of Flares in PKS 0903-57

Study of gamma-ray flares using Fermi-LAT data with detailed temporal and spectral analysis, constraining jet parameters through SED modeling.

Research Areas

Multi-wavelength Data Analysis: Analysis of gamma-ray, X-ray, and optical/UV data to study variability, spectral evolution, and flaring behavior of AGNs.

Spectral Energy Distribution Modelling: Leptonic modeling of AGN emission using tools such as GAMERA, JetSeT, and Naima.

Neutrino–Gamma-ray Connection: Investigation of the possible association between high-energy neutrinos and blazar emission.

Quasi-Periodic Oscillations: Time-series analysis to identify periodic signals and probe jet dynamics.

Cosmic Ray Simulations: Simulation of ultra-high energy cosmic ray propagation and electromagnetic cascades.
