Spectral Modeling of Flares in PKS 0903-57

In this work, we present a detailed timing and spectral study of the blazar PKS 0903-57, aimed at understanding the physical processes driving its variability. Through a systematic timing analysis of long-term gamma-ray observations, we identify distinct flaring episodes, along with comparatively low-flux states before and after each event, which we refer to as the pre-flare and post-flare phases.

Broadband SED modelling of one of the flaring states of PKS 0903-57.

By modeling the gamma-ray peaks, we estimate the rise and decay times of individual flares, allowing us to explore possible patterns in their temporal evolution. These timescales provide valuable insight into the interplay between particle acceleration and radiative cooling mechanisms within the jet. In addition, we derive the characteristic variability timescale, which places important constraints on the size and location of the emission region.

Building on this, we perform a spectral analysis focused on the flaring states. Our modeling suggests that a one-zone leptonic scenario can provide a consistent description of the observed emission, offering a plausible framework for interpreting the underlying radiation processes. However, a more complete picture will require broader and truly simultaneous multi-wavelength coverage.

The multi-wavelength light curves constructed in this study highlight both the strengths and current limitations of available data, underscoring the importance of coordinated observations in advancing our understanding of relativistic jet physics.
