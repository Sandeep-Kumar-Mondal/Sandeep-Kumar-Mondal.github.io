Multi-wavelength Study of Markarian 180 (Mrk 180)

In this work, we present a timing and detailed spectral investigation of the high-frequency-peaked BL Lac object Mrk 180, based on long-term X-ray and gamma-ray observations. Examination of the 30-day binned Fermi-LAT gamma-ray light curve reveals that the source remains largely steady over time, with no clear evidence of prominent flaring episodes. This limits the scope for detailed temporal analysis.

Leptonic + hadronic (pp) modeling of the multi-wavelength SED of Mrk 180. The grey-shaded region represents the difference between attenuated and unattenuated emission components.

We therefore focus on constructing the long-term multi-wavelength spectral energy distribution (SED). Our modelling shows that a leptohadronic (pp) scenario provides a consistent and physically motivated description of the emission across energy bands, capturing both the low- and high-energy components of the spectrum.

Finally, by comparing our simulation results with current observational constraints, we assess the potential role of Mrk 180 as a source of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs). Under conservative assumptions for extragalactic magnetic field strengths, we find that Mrk 180 is unlikely to contribute significantly to the Telescope Array (TA) hotspot.
